## Design Context

### Users

Developers using TIA Studio as a local-first desktop command center for running Pi coding-agent conversations against real workspaces, reviewing activity, and monitoring scheduled automations. They need dense information to stay legible during long work sessions and important execution controls to remain close to the composer.

### Brand Personality

Focused, capable, and calm. The product should feel like a trustworthy native developer tool: direct language, quiet confidence, and no ornamental technology theater.

### Aesthetic Direction

A restrained dark desktop interface inspired by the information hierarchy of the Codex desktop app: compact navigation, border-led structure, strong alignment, and progressive disclosure. Preserve the existing near-black neutral palette and sans-serif typography. Avoid oversized editorial headings, blue decorative washes, nested cards, gradients, and status chrome that repeats information available at the point of action.

### Design Principles

- Put execution choices where the action happens: model and permission controls belong in the composer.
- Keep the conversation canvas quiet; thread naming is agent-owned and shell status should not consume permanent space.
- Prefer native desktop split views, rows, and separators over card galleries.
- Make selected, running, paused, loading, and error states unmistakable without relying on decoration.
- Keep controls keyboard accessible, compact, and truthful to the runtime capabilities.
